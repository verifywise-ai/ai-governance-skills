# Conformity assessment procedures

## Assessment path determination

| AI system type | Assessment path | Annex | Key requirement |
|---------------|-----------------|-------|-----------------|
| Biometric identification systems (Annex III §1) | Notified body assessment | Annex VII (quality management system + technical documentation assessment) | Mandatory third-party assessment |
| All other high-risk AI systems (Annex III §2-8) | Self-assessment (internal control) | Annex VI | Provider conducts assessment internally |
| High-risk AI systems covered by EU harmonisation legislation in Annex I Section A | Follow the conformity assessment procedure under that legislation | As specified in relevant sectoral legislation | AI Act requirements integrated into existing sectoral assessment |

## Self-assessment — Internal control (Annex VI)

| Step | Action | Detail | Evidence |
|------|--------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Verify quality management system | Confirm the QMS complies with Article 17 requirements | QMS documentation; internal audit results |
| 2 | Examine technical documentation | Verify that technical documentation meets Annex IV requirements and assess that the system was designed and developed in compliance with Articles 8-15 | Annex IV compliant technical documentation; design verification records |
| 3 | Verify monitoring controls | Confirm post-market monitoring system (Article 72) is in place and functioning | Post-market monitoring plan; data collection procedures |
| 4 | Draw up EU Declaration of Conformity | Complete the declaration per Article 47 and Annex V, covering each AI system | Signed EU Declaration of Conformity per Annex V format |
| 5 | Affix CE marking | Apply CE marking to the AI system or its documentation per Article 48 | CE marking applied; documentation updated |

## Notified body assessment (Annex VII)

### Quality management system assessment

| Step | Action | Detail | Evidence |
|------|--------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Submit application | Apply to a notified body of the provider's choice; include QMS documentation and technical documentation for one representative AI system per type | Application form; QMS documentation; representative system technical documentation |
| 2 | QMS evaluation | Notified body assesses the QMS against Article 17 requirements | Notified body QMS assessment report |
| 3 | Technical documentation review | Notified body examines technical documentation to verify compliance with Articles 8-15 | Notified body technical documentation assessment report |
| 4 | Decision and certificate | Notified body issues quality management system approval certificate (valid maximum 5 years, renewable) or rejects with detailed reasons | QMS approval certificate or rejection letter with reasons |

### Surveillance

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Purpose | Ensure provider maintains and applies the approved QMS |
| Frequency | Periodic audits by the notified body |
| Scope | QMS implementation; changes to the system; post-market monitoring data |
| Provider obligation | Inform the notified body of any intended changes to the QMS or the AI system that may affect compliance |
| Non-compliance | Notified body may restrict, suspend, or withdraw the certificate |

## EU Declaration of Conformity (Article 47)

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Provider identification | Name and address of the provider |
| AI system identification | Name, type, and any additional unambiguous reference allowing identification |
| Statement | Declaration that the AI system is in conformity with the EU AI Act and any other relevant EU legislation |
| Harmonised standards | Reference to harmonised standards or common specifications used |
| Notified body (if applicable) | Name and identification number of the notified body, description of the procedure performed, certificate reference |
| Date and signature | Place and date of issue, name and function of person signing, signature |
| Responsibility statement | Statement that the declaration is drawn up under the sole responsibility of the provider |
| Kept up to date | Must be continuously updated and kept for 10 years after the AI system is placed on the market |
| Language | Made available in a language required by the Member State where the system is placed on market or put into service |

## CE marking (Article 48)

| Aspect | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| Visibility | Must be visible, legible, and indelible |
| Placement | Affixed to the high-risk AI system or, where not possible, to its packaging or accompanying documentation |
| Digital marking | Permitted; must be accessible via the AI system interface or through a QR code or equivalent |
| Timing | Affixed before the AI system is placed on the market |
| National rules | Subject to existing rules on CE marking under Regulation (EC) No 765/2008 |
| Misleading markings | No other markings or inscriptions likely to mislead third parties as to the meaning or form of CE marking |

## Post-market monitoring (Article 72)

| Aspect | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| System establishment | Providers must establish and document a post-market monitoring system in a manner proportionate to the nature and risks of the AI system |
| Plan | Post-market monitoring plan must be part of the technical documentation (Annex IV) |
| Data collection | Actively and systematically collect, document, and analyse relevant data from deployers or other sources throughout the AI system lifecycle |
| Deployer cooperation | Deployers must provide data to the provider upon request |
| Analysis purpose | Evaluate continuous compliance with Articles 8-15; identify needed corrective or preventive actions |
| Serious incidents | Feed into incident reporting obligations under Article 73 |
| Authority access | Make findings available to market surveillance authorities upon request |
