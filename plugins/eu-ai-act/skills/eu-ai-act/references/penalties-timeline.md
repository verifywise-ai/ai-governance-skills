# EU AI Act penalties and enforcement timeline

## Phased enforcement timeline

| Date | Milestone | Key provisions | Who is affected |
|------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| August 1, 2024 | Entry into force | Regulation published and entered into force | All entities — begin preparation |
| February 2, 2025 | Prohibited AI practices apply | Article 5 prohibitions enforceable; AI literacy obligations (Article 4) apply | All providers and deployers — must cease prohibited practices |
| August 2, 2025 | GPAI obligations + governance | Chapter V (GPAI model obligations) applies; AI Office operational; codes of practice finalized; notified body designation rules apply | GPAI model providers; notified bodies; Member States |
| August 2, 2026 | High-risk AI + conformity assessment | Chapter III (high-risk requirements Articles 8-15) applies; conformity assessment (Annexes VI-VII) required; CE marking required; registration in EU database required; deployer obligations (Article 26-27) apply | High-risk AI providers and deployers; importers; distributors |
| August 2, 2027 | Annex I product obligations | Obligations for high-risk AI systems that are safety components of products covered by EU harmonisation legislation listed in Annex I Section A | Product manufacturers integrating AI into regulated products (machinery, medical devices, toys, etc.) |

## Penalty structure

| Violation type | Maximum fine (entity) | Maximum fine (SME/startup) | Article |
|---------------|----------------------|---------------------------|---------|
| Prohibited AI practices (Article 5) | 35,000,000 EUR or 7% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher | Lower of the two amounts applies proportionately | Art. 99(3) |
| Non-compliance with high-risk requirements (Articles 8-15, provider/deployer obligations) | 15,000,000 EUR or 3% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher | Lower of the two amounts applies proportionately | Art. 99(4) |
| Supplying incorrect, incomplete, or misleading information to authorities | 7,500,000 EUR or 1% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher | Lower of the two amounts applies proportionately | Art. 99(5) |
| Non-compliance by EU institutions, bodies, offices, and agencies | Up to 1,500,000 EUR (prohibited practices), 750,000 EUR (other violations) | N/A | Art. 99(6) |

## Penalty considerations

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Proportionality | Penalties must be effective, proportionate, and dissuasive |
| SME/startup protections | Fines capped at proportionately lower amounts for SMEs and startups |
| Factors in determination | Nature, gravity, and duration of infringement; intentional or negligent character; actions taken to mitigate; size and market share; previous infringements; degree of cooperation with authorities; manner in which infringement became known |
| Turnover calculation | Based on total worldwide annual turnover of the preceding financial year; for group entities, the turnover of the ultimate parent undertaking |
| Member State enforcement | Each Member State lays down rules on penalties applicable to infringements by operators; may include non-monetary penalties (warnings, compliance orders) |

## Enforcement bodies

| Body | Role | Scope |
|------|------|-------|
| AI Office (European Commission) | Oversight of GPAI models; development of codes of practice; monitoring systemic risk models | EU-wide; GPAI-specific enforcement |
| National competent authorities | Market surveillance; enforcement of high-risk AI obligations; investigation and penalty imposition | Member State level; all AI Act provisions except GPAI |
| National market surveillance authorities | Monitoring AI systems on the market; product safety enforcement | Member State level; market-placed AI systems |
| European AI Board | Coordination between Member States; consistency of application; advisory role | EU-wide; advisory and coordination |
| Notified bodies | Conformity assessment for biometric AI systems; surveillance audits | Designated by Member States; Annex VII procedures |

## Key deadlines for organizations

| Deadline | Action required | Risk of non-compliance |
|----------|----------------|----------------------|
| Before February 2, 2025 | Audit AI systems against Article 5 prohibited list; cease any prohibited practices; implement AI literacy measures | Up to 35M EUR / 7% turnover for prohibited practice violations |
| Before August 2, 2025 | GPAI model providers: prepare Annex XI documentation; establish copyright policy; publish training content summary; prepare downstream provider information | Up to 15M EUR / 3% turnover |
| Before August 2, 2026 | High-risk AI providers: complete conformity assessment; prepare technical documentation (Annex IV); establish QMS; register in EU database; affix CE marking. Deployers: establish human oversight; prepare FRIA where required | Up to 15M EUR / 3% turnover |
| Before August 2, 2027 | Product manufacturers: ensure AI safety components in Annex I products comply with integrated conformity assessment requirements | Up to 15M EUR / 3% turnover |
