# South Korea AI Basic Act — Transparency requirements

## General AI disclosure obligations

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| When AI is used | Users and affected persons must be informed that AI is being used in decisions or interactions that affect them |
| What to disclose | Purpose of the AI system, type of decisions it makes or supports, the entity responsible for its deployment |
| How to disclose | In a clear, accessible, and understandable format appropriate to the context and audience |
| When to disclose | Before or at the point of interaction; before a decision is communicated |
| Who must disclose | Primarily the deployer; developers must enable disclosure mechanisms |
| Language | Disclosure must be provided in Korean for services targeting Korean residents |

## Deepfake and synthetic content labeling

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| AI-generated content | Must be clearly labeled as AI-generated at the point of creation and distribution |
| Deepfakes (synthetic media) | Synthetic audio, video, or images depicting real persons must include visible or audible labeling |
| Substantially modified content | Content substantially modified by AI (not merely filtered or minor edits) must be labeled |
| Label format | Labels must be clear, conspicuous, and understandable to the average person |
| Machine-readable marking | AI-generated content should include machine-readable metadata indicating AI generation (specific standards to be defined) |
| Label persistence | Labels must remain attached through redistribution and sharing |
| Exceptions | Clearly artistic or satirical use may be subject to modified requirements (specific guidelines to be issued by MSIT) |
| Removal prohibition | Users must not remove, obscure, or falsify AI-generated content labels |

## Right to explanation

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| When it applies | When an AI system makes or substantially contributes to a significant automated decision affecting an individual's rights or interests |
| What must be explained | The general logic of the AI system, the key factors that influenced the specific decision, and how to contest the decision |
| Who must provide | The deployer of the AI system (developer must design systems to enable explanations) |
| Who may request | Any individual affected by a significant automated decision |
| Timeframe | Within a reasonable period of the request (specific timeframes may be set by presidential decree) |
| Format | In clear, plain language understandable to the requesting individual |
| Significant decision examples | Denial of credit, employment decisions, insurance pricing, public benefit eligibility, law enforcement actions |
| Limitations | Explanation must not compromise trade secrets or intellectual property; however, it must still be sufficiently meaningful |

## Notification requirements by AI system type

| AI system type | Notification required | Timing | Method |
|---------------|----------------------|--------|--------|
| Chatbot / conversational AI | Disclose that the user is interacting with an AI system, not a human | Before or at the start of interaction | Clear text or audio disclosure |
| Decision-support AI | Disclose that AI is involved in or contributing to the decision | Before the decision is communicated | Written notice accompanying the decision |
| Autonomous decision AI | Disclose that the decision is made by an AI system; provide explanation mechanism | Before or with the decision | Written notice with explanation and appeal information |
| Content generation AI | Label outputs as AI-generated | At the point of content creation or distribution | Visible label on content; machine-readable metadata |
| Biometric AI | Specific consent and disclosure before biometric data is collected and processed by AI | Before data collection | Explicit consent mechanism; written notice |
| Recommendation AI | Disclose that recommendations are AI-generated and the basis of recommendations | At point of recommendation | Visible disclosure in the user interface |
| Monitoring / surveillance AI | Disclose that AI monitoring is in use and what is being monitored | Before individuals enter monitored space or context | Posted notices; written policy |

## Transparency obligations by role

| Role | Obligations |
|------|------------|
| AI Developer | Design systems to enable transparency (provide documentation of system capabilities, limitations, and intended use; build in explanation capabilities for high-risk systems; provide labeling infrastructure for content-generating AI) |
| AI Deployer | Implement transparency in operations (notify affected persons of AI use; provide explanations upon request; ensure deepfake labels are applied; maintain records of disclosures made) |
| AI User | Preserve transparency (do not conceal AI involvement when disclosure is required; do not remove AI-generated content labels; do not represent AI outputs as purely human-generated in contexts requiring disclosure) |
